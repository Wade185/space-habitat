Aitne AI Assistant

Discovery: 2001 by Scott S. Sheppard and team

Parent Planet: Jupiter

Size: ~3 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~628 million km

Description: Aitne is one of Jupiter’s small irregular moons, part of the Carme group. It follows a retrograde orbit, meaning it travels opposite Jupiter’s rotation.

Fun Fact: Aitne was named after a mythological nymph associated with Mount Etna in Sicily.

Breathing / Oxygen

No natural atmosphere exists. Oxygen must be produced through ice electrolysis or habitat recycling systems.

Water & Sewage

Local resources are scarce. Colonists would need to import or harvest ice from nearby Jovian moons and recycle water completely.

Food Production

Closed-loop hydroponics, algae farming, and bioreactors would supply nutrition inside shielded domes.

Energy

Solar energy is very weak at Jupiter’s distance. Nuclear fission reactors would be essential, with future fusion as an option.

Shelter

Habitats must have heavy radiation shielding, since Jupiter’s magnetosphere is extremely harsh. Underground bases or regolith domes are preferred.
