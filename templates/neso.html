Discovery: 2002, by Matthew Holman and team

Parent Planet: Neptune

Size: ~60 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~4.5 billion km (varies)

Description: Neso is Neptune’s most distant known moon, with an extremely elongated orbit, taking 27 years to complete one lap around Neptune.

Fun Fact: It holds the record for the most distant moon orbiting any planet in our solar system.

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere. Oxygen must be fully produced and recycled.

Water & Sewage

No water confirmed. Colonies must recycle or import water.

Food Production

Hydroponic and greenhouse farming in sealed bases required.

Energy

Solar power is impractical. Nuclear energy is essential.

Shelter

Radiation and extreme cold require protected underground shelters.
