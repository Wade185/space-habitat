Kerberos — Habitat Assistant

Discovery: 2011 by the Hubble Space Telescope Pluto Companion Search Team

Parent Planet: Pluto

Size: ~19 km across (irregular shape)

Distance from Earth: ~4.9 billion km

Description: Kerberos is a small moon of Pluto with a double-lobed shape, resembling a peanut. Its surface is thought to be coated in water ice, giving it a reflective appearance.

Fun Fact: Kerberos is named after the multi-headed dog from Greek mythology that guards the underworld.

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere. Any settlement would need sealed life-support habitats to generate oxygen.

Water & Sewage

Kerberos likely has surface water ice. This could be harvested and melted for use, with closed-loop recycling systems handling sewage.

Food Production

No natural resources. Colonists must grow food in pressurized hydroponic or aeroponic systems under artificial lighting.

Energy

Solar energy is very weak at Pluto’s distance. Nuclear fission or compact fusion would be the only practical options.

Shelter

Habitats would need thick shielding or underground construction to protect from radiation and micrometeoroid impacts.
