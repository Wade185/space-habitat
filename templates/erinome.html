Erinome — Habitat Assistant

Discovery: 2000 by Scott S. Sheppard and team

Parent Planet: Jupiter

Size: ~3 km diameter (estimated)

Distance from Earth: varies — roughly ~600 million km (depends on orbital positions)

Description: Erinome is a small, retrograde irregular moon of Jupiter and a member of the Carme group. Its orbit is inclined and distant compared to Jupiter's major moons; it likely originated from a captured object.

Fun Fact: Erinome orbits Jupiter in a retrograde direction (opposite to the planet's rotation).

Breathing / Oxygen

Erinome does not have an atmosphere. Any human presence would require pressurized habitats with closed-loop life support systems that generate and recycle oxygen continuously.

Water & Sewage

No exposed liquid water or surface ice is known. Missions would need to either import water from larger nearby bodies or rely on extremely efficient recycling (e.g., ISS-style water reclamation) and potential in-situ resource extraction if subsurface ice is discovered.

Food Production

All food production must be done inside sealed agricultural modules using hydroponics, aeroponics, or lab-grown alternatives. Waste streams should be converted and reused to close nutrient loops.

Energy

Erinome is far from the Sun, so solar is weak. The most practical energy sources for long-term bases would be compact nuclear fission reactors (or fusion in future tech scenarios) combined with battery/thermal storage for peak loads.

Shelter & Radiation

Because Erinome orbits Jupiter, radiation from Jupiter's magnetosphere is a major concern. Habitats must be shielded — preferably subterranean or covered with regolith/lead-equivalent shielding — to reduce radiation exposure and micrometeorite risk.
