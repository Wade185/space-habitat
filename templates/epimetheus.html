Parent Planet: Saturn

Size: ~113 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~1.27 billion km

Description: Epimetheus is a small, irregularly shaped moon of Saturn that shares nearly the same orbit as Janus. The two moons swap orbits every four years in a unique orbital dance.

Fun Fact: Epimetheus and Janus are called "co-orbital moons" — the only such pair in the solar system.

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere, no oxygen. Breathing requires sealed life-support systems.

Water & Sewage

Food Production

Energy

Solar is weak at Saturn’s distance. Nuclear reactors are the main option.

Due to low gravity and lack of atmosphere, habitats would be built inside pressurized domes or underground modules.
