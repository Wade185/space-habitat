Metis AI Assistant

Discovery: 1979, by Stephen P. Synnott (Voyager 1 images)

Parent Planet: Jupiter

Size: ~60 km across

Distance from Earth: ~628 million km

Description: Metis is the innermost moon of Jupiter, orbiting within its rings. Its small size and proximity to Jupiter make it an extreme environment.

Fun Fact: Metis helps supply material to Jupiter’s main ring through dust shed from its surface.

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere. Colonists must bring and recycle oxygen.

Water & Sewage

No natural water. Supplies would come from ice mined from nearby moons like Europa or Ganymede.

Food Production

Only possible in closed biospheres with hydroponics and synthetic food production.

Energy

Solar energy is weak at Jupiter’s distance. Nuclear reactors would be the primary power source.

Shelter

Structures must be shielded heavily to survive Jupiter’s deadly radiation.
