Chaldene AI Assistant

Discovery: 2000, by Scott S. Sheppard and team

Parent Planet: Jupiter

Size: ~4 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~628 million km (varies)

Description: Chaldene is a small retrograde irregular moon of Jupiter, part of the Carme group. Its orbit is tilted and elongated, suggesting it is a captured object.

Fun Fact: Chaldene and its Carme group companions likely originated from a single captured asteroid that fragmented.

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere exists. Habitats must generate oxygen via electrolysis of imported ice or recycling systems.

Water & Sewage

No local water confirmed. Colonies would need imports from outer moons or captured comets. Recycling is essential.

Food Production

Food production requires closed hydroponic/aeroponic systems with artificial lighting inside pressurized bases.

Energy

Solar is weak at Jupiter’s distance; nuclear power is the most reliable source.

Shelter

Underground or regolith-covered habitats would protect against cosmic radiation and impacts.
