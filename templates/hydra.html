Hydra — Habitat Assistant

Discovery: 2005 by the Hubble Space Telescope Pluto Companion Search Team

Parent Planet: Pluto (dwarf planet)

Size: ~51 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~5.9 billion km (varies)

Description: Hydra is one of Pluto’s small outer moons. It has a highly reflective surface made mostly of water ice and is irregular in shape. Hydra rotates chaotically, meaning its spin is unpredictable.

Fun Fact: Hydra reflects more sunlight than Pluto itself, due to its icy bright surface.

Breathing / Oxygen

Hydra has no atmosphere. Any settlement would need sealed life-support systems for oxygen supply and recycling.

Water & Sewage

Hydra’s icy surface could provide water for colonists. Ice can be harvested and purified, with sewage recycled into usable water.

Food Production

No natural food sources exist. Colonists must establish hydroponic or aquaponic farms inside controlled environments.

Energy

Like other Plutonian moons, solar power is very weak. Nuclear power plants or RTGs would be the main reliable energy sources.

Shelter

Habitats must be built underground or shielded with thick ice walls to protect against radiation, extreme cold, and micrometeoroid impacts.
