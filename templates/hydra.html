Hydra AI Assistant

Discovery: 2005, by Hubble Space Telescope team (Showalter, Weaver, Stern, and others)

Parent Planet: Pluto

Size: ~51 km diameter (irregular)

Distance from Earth: ~4.8–7.5 billion km (varies)

Description: Hydra is one of Pluto’s small outer moons. Its irregular shape and reflective surface suggest it is covered in water ice.

Fun Fact: Hydra is named after the nine-headed serpent from Greek mythology, a companion to Pluto (Hades).

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere. Any oxygen must be created from water ice through electrolysis or transported from Earth.

Water & Sewage

Hydra’s icy surface could provide water for colonists after mining, purification, and recycling.

Food Production

Like Pluto’s other moons, food production would rely on hydroponics or bioreactors inside sealed habitats.

Energy

Solar power is nearly useless at this distance. Nuclear reactors would be the only reliable source of power.

Shelter

Habitats must be shielded underground or inside ice caverns to protect from radiation and micrometeorites.
