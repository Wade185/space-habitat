Discovery: 1990 by Mark Showalter (Voyager images)

Parent Planet: Saturn

Size: ~28 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~1.27 billion km

Description: Pan is a tiny moon located inside Saturn’s A-ring, responsible for creating and maintaining the Encke Gap.

Fun Fact: Pan looks like a “flying saucer” because of equatorial ridges formed by ring material.

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere or oxygen. Sealed habitats with life-support are required.

Water & Sewage

No surface water. Ice could be extracted from Saturn’s rings or imported from Enceladus.

Food Production

Food would be grown in hydroponics or transported from Earth initially.

Energy

Solar energy is weak. Nuclear reactors are best.

Shelter

Small bases inside protective domes, shielded from radiation, would be necessary.
