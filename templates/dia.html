Dia AI Assistant

Discovery: 2000, by the Spacewatch survey

Parent Planet: Jupiter

Size: ~4 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~588 million km (varies)

Description: Dia is one of Jupiter’s small outer irregular moons, belonging to the Himalia group. It follows a distant, inclined orbit around Jupiter.

Fun Fact: Dia is named after a daughter of Deion in Greek mythology. It is so small and faint that it can only be observed with powerful telescopes.

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere, no oxygen. Breathing requires artificial life-support systems.

Water & Sewage

No evidence of water. Colonists must import water or recycle it entirely within habitats.

Food Production

Food would need to be grown in hydroponic or aeroponic systems inside sealed habitats.

Energy

Solar power is weak so far from the Sun. Nuclear reactors are the best option.

Shelter

Habitats would need to be fully pressurized domes or underground modules protected from radiation.
