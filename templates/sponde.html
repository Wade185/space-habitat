Sponde AI Assistant

Discovery: 1979 by Stephen P. Synnott

Parent Planet: Jupiter

Size: ~2 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~628 million km (on average)

Description: Sponde is one of Jupiter’s small irregular moons, part of the Pasiphae group. It has a dark, irregular surface and orbits far from the planet.

Fun Fact: The moon is named after one of the Horae, Greek goddesses of the seasons.

Breathing / Oxygen

Sponde has no atmosphere. Colonists would need fully sealed habitats.

Water & Sewage

No surface water exists. Ice would need to be imported from other Jovian moons.

Food Production

Food must be grown in hydroponic or aeroponic greenhouses using imported nutrients.

Energy

Solar power is very weak at this distance; nuclear energy is the most reliable option.

Shelter

Habitats must be dome-based or underground with radiation shielding due to Jupiter’s strong magnetic field.
