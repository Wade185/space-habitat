Ceres AI Assistant

Discovery: 1801, by Giuseppe Piazzi

Parent Region: Asteroid Belt (between Mars and Jupiter)

Size: ~940 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~414 million km (average)

Description: Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt and the only dwarf planet located there. It has a mixture of rock and ice, and evidence suggests salty liquid water exists beneath its crust.

Fun Fact: Ceres was the first asteroid ever discovered and is now classified as a dwarf planet.

Breathing / Oxygen

Ceres has a very thin exosphere of water vapor. Human habitats would require sealed environments with oxygen produced from water electrolysis or recycling systems.

Water & Sewage

Ceres is rich in water ice. Colonies could mine ice, melt it, and recycle wastewater in a closed loop.

Food Production

Hydroponic and aeroponic farms inside shielded domes or underground caverns could provide sustainable crops.

Energy

Solar energy is feasible in the asteroid belt, but nuclear power would ensure reliable and continuous electricity.

Shelter

Habitats could be built in underground caverns or regolith-covered domes for protection from radiation and micrometeoroids.
