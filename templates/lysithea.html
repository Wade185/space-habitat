Lysithea AI Assistant

Discovery: 1938, by Seth Barnes Nicholson

Parent Planet: Jupiter

Size: ~42 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~628 million km (varies)

Description: Lysithea is a prograde irregular moon of Jupiter and part of the Himalia group. It likely formed from a captured asteroid that fragmented long ago.

Fun Fact: Despite being small, Lysithea’s brightness made it detectable with 1930s telescopes—rare for irregular moons.

Breathing / Oxygen

No natural oxygen. Colonists must rely on electrolysis of ice or closed-loop recycling systems.

Water & Sewage

Water is unlikely to be present. Imports from larger moons like Europa would be necessary, with full recycling.

Food Production

Hydroponic greenhouses would provide food in pressurized environments with artificial light.

Energy

Solar energy is weak at Jupiter’s distance. Compact nuclear reactors or advanced fusion prototypes would be used.

Shelter

Radiation protection is crucial near Jupiter—colonies must be underground or shielded with thick regolith walls.
