Charon — Habitat Assistant

Discovery: 1978 by James Christy and Robert Harrington

Parent Planet: Pluto (dwarf planet)

Size: ~1,212 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~5.9 billion km (varies)

Description: Charon is the largest moon of Pluto and is so big compared to Pluto that the two are sometimes considered a double dwarf planet system. Its surface is covered in water ice, with signs of past geological activity.

Fun Fact: Charon always shows the same face to Pluto, and Pluto also shows the same face to Charon — they are tidally locked to each other.

Breathing / Oxygen

Charon has no atmosphere. Habitats must supply and recycle oxygen with sealed life-support systems.

Water & Sewage

Charon’s surface contains abundant water ice, which can be harvested and purified. Sewage and wastewater should be recycled for sustainable living.

Food Production

No native sources of food. Colonies would rely on hydroponic farms and artificial lighting to grow crops within controlled environments.

Energy

Solar power is extremely weak at Pluto’s distance. Nuclear reactors or radioisotope power systems would be the main sources of energy.

Shelter

Colonies must be underground or shielded with thick ice/regolith to protect from cosmic radiation and extreme cold.
