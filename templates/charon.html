Charon AI Assistant

Discovery: 1978, by James Christy

Parent Planet: Pluto

Size: ~1,212 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~4.8–7.5 billion km (varies)

Description: Charon is Pluto’s largest moon, so big that Pluto–Charon is often considered a double-dwarf-planet system. Its surface is covered with water ice and it has striking red polar regions caused by methane from Pluto’s atmosphere.

Fun Fact: Charon is about half the size of Pluto, making their relationship unique in the Solar System. They orbit a point outside Pluto’s surface.

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere to breathe. Oxygen would need to be generated from water ice through electrolysis.

Water & Sewage

Charon’s surface has abundant water ice. This could be mined, purified, and recycled within habitats.

Food Production

Hydroponic and bioreactor farming would be necessary within domes to sustain colonies.

Energy

Solar power is too weak this far out. Colonists would rely on nuclear fission or fusion reactors.

Shelter

Habitats would likely be built underground or shielded with ice/regolith to protect against cosmic radiation.
