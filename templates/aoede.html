Aoede AI Assistant

Discovery: 2003, by Scott S. Sheppard and team

Parent Planet: Jupiter

Size: ~4 km diameter (approx.)

Distance from Earth: ~628 million km (varies with orbital positions)

Description: Aoede is a small, irregular, retrograde moon of Jupiter. It belongs to one of Jupiter’s groups of outer irregular satellites and follows a tilted, elongated orbit.

Fun Fact: Aoede travels in a retrograde orbit (opposite Jupiter’s rotation) and is one of many tiny captured objects orbiting the giant planet.

Breathing / Oxygen

Aoede has no atmosphere. Any human presence would require fully sealed habitats with oxygen produced by electrolysis of imported ice, recycled air systems, or ISRU from nearby icy bodies.

Water & Sewage

Water would be a precious resource. Sources include importing from larger Jovian moons or harvesting ice from outer-system bodies, then using closed-loop purification and recycling for all wastewater.

Food Production

Food would be grown in controlled hydroponic/aeroponic bays inside shielded habitats. Algae, insect protein, and lab-grown meat could supplement crops to maximize efficiency and nutrition.

Energy

Solar power is limited at Jupiter’s distance. Reliable base power would come from nuclear fission reactors or compact advanced reactors; small solar arrays could be used for low-power systems or as backups.

Shelter

Habitats must be heavily shielded from micrometeoroids and cosmic radiation. Preferred solutions are subsurface caverns, regolith-covered modules, or heavily armored pressurized domes.
