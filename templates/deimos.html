Deimos AI Assistant

Discovery: 1877, by Asaph Hall

Parent Planet: Mars

Size: ~12.4 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~78–400 million km (depending on Mars’s orbit)

Description: Deimos is the smaller and outermost moon of Mars. It has a smooth, dusty surface covered by loose regolith.

Fun Fact: Deimos means “terror” in Greek and is named after the twin brother of Phobos, both companions of the god Ares (Mars).

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere exists. Colonists would need habitats with full life support and oxygen supplies.

Water & Sewage

Deimos has no liquid water, but its regolith may contain traces of ice. Recycling and imports would be necessary.

Food Production

Hydroponic systems inside pressurized greenhouses could provide sustainable food.

Energy

Solar energy is strong near Mars, making solar panels viable. Nuclear power could provide backup.

Shelter

Habitats would be built underground or shielded with regolith to protect against radiation and impacts.
