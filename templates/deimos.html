Deimos — Habitat Assistant

Discovery: 1877 by Asaph Hall

Parent Planet: Mars

Size: ~12.4 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~78 million km (when Mars is closest)

Description: Deimos is the smaller and outermost of Mars’s two moons. It is an irregularly shaped body with a dusty surface, covered by a thick regolith layer.

Fun Fact: Because Deimos orbits farther out than Phobos, it is slowly drifting away from Mars.

Breathing / Oxygen

Deimos has no atmosphere or oxygen. Colonists would require closed life-support systems to survive.

Water & Sewage

Deimos may contain water ice deep beneath its regolith. Extracted water would need purification, and sewage systems must recycle all resources.

Food Production

Deimos has no natural food. Food would come from hydroponic or aquaponic greenhouses inside domed habitats.

Energy

Being closer to the Sun than outer moons, Deimos can use solar panels effectively, but nuclear power would still provide reliability during long Mars eclipses.

Shelter

Colonists must build bases underground or in thick domes to protect against radiation, low gravity, and micrometeoroids.
