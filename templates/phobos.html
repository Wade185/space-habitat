Phobos AI Assistant

Discovery: 1877 by Asaph Hall

Parent Planet: Mars

Size: ~22 km diameter

Distance from Earth: ~78 million km (at closest)

Description: Phobos is the larger of Mars’s two moons, small and irregularly shaped, and orbits extremely close to Mars.

Fun Fact: Phobos is slowly spiraling inward and may break apart in ~50 million years.

Breathing / Oxygen

No atmosphere. Colonies need airtight domes with oxygen recycling.

Water & Sewage

Water may be mined from Martian ice deposits nearby, but Phobos itself is dry.

Food Production

No native food. Hydroponic greenhouses needed for colonists.

Energy

Solar power is feasible due to Mars’ proximity to the Sun, but nuclear backup is ideal.

Shelter

Phobos has almost no gravity. Colonies would be anchored habitats, shielded from radiation.
